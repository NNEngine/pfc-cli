# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of **pfc-cli** pledge to make participation in our project a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

We are committed to fostering a welcoming and professional environment.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in language and actions
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get started
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others’ private information without permission
- Any behavior that disrupts or undermines a professional environment


## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, issues, or other participation that violates this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues and pull requests
- Discussions
- Community interactions related to the project

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainer(s).

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers may take appropriate corrective action, including temporary or permanent bans from participation in the project.


## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

1. **Correction** – A private warning explaining the violation
2. **Warning** – A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior
3. **Temporary Ban** – Temporary removal from project participation
4. **Permanent Ban** – Permanent removal for severe or repeated violations
